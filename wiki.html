Beyblade Wiki is a popular Japanese multimedia franchise centered on customizable
spinning tops used in battles within a stadium, known as Beystadiums, launched by Takara Tomy in 1999, gaining global
fame through manga, anime, and various toy series like Metal Fusion and Burst, featuring attack, defense, stamina, and balance types,
with battles decided by knocking opponents out or causing them to stop spinning. Core Concept
Toys: High-performance spinning tops with interchangeable parts (Energy Layer, Forge Disc, Performance Tip, etc.).

Gameplay: Players launch their Beyblades into a Beystadium, aiming to out-spin, burst (break apart), or knock out the opponent's top.

Origin: Inspired by traditional Japanese beigoma and developed by Takara (now Takara Tomy), launched with a manga in 1999.

The Beyblade OG series is the original anime and includes three seasons: Beyblade, Beyblade: V-Force, and Beyblade: G-Revolution.
It aired from 2001 to 2003 and follows the story of a group

Metal Fight (Metal Saga): Introduced metal parts, new launchers, and themes around Bit Beasts.
The Beyblade Metal Series (or Metal Saga) is a popular anime and manga spin-off featuring new characters, including protagonist Gingka Hagane,
battling with constellation-themed metal spinning tops,
focusing on individual fights and upgrading Beys with different parts,
all while confronting the evil Dark Nebula organization and powerful rivals like Ryuga.

It's divided into four seasons: Metal Fusion, Metal Masters, Metal Fury, and Shogun Steel, introducing new Beyblade systems and legendary figures across its run.
Key Aspects:
Protagonist: Gingka Hagane, a talented Blader with his powerful Storm Pegasus.
Main Goal: Defeat the Dark Nebula organization, who stole the forbidden Bey Lightning L-Drago, and uncover Gingka's past.
Gameplay: Emphasizes individual battles, part customization, and the power of the "Blader's Spirit".
Mythology: Beys are linked to constellations, and later seasons introduce mystical elements like the "Star Fragment".
The Seasons (Metal Saga):
Beyblade: Metal Fusion (2009-2010): Introduces Gingka, his friends, and the fight against Dark Nebula.
Beyblade: Metal Masters (2010-2011): Features Gingka and friends competing in a World Championship.
Beyblade: Metal Fury (2011-2012): Gingka and Legendary Bladers seek to defeat the God of Destruction, Nemesis.
Beyblade: Shogun Steel (2012-2013): A new cast takes over, set years later, but old characters return.

Beyblade Burst: Features "bursting" as a key win condition, with Energy Layers, Forge Discs, and Performance Tips.

Beyblade Burst is a popular Japanese manga, toyline (spinning tops called Beyblades), and anime franchise by Hiro Morita, known for its high-energy battles where Beyblades
can "burst" apart, featuring characters like Valt Aoi
aiming to become world champions, with multiple anime seasons (Evolution, Turbo, Rise, Surge, QuadDrive, QuadStrike) introducing
new mechanics and Beys, available globally through Takara Tomy and Hasbro.
Key Aspects:
Story: Follows passionate Bladers, primarily Valt Aoi, as they train, battle, and compete in tournaments, focusing on friendship, rivalry, and self-improvement.
Mechanics: Introduces the "Burst System," allowing Beyblades to break apart mid-battle for a "burst finish," a key innovation over previous Beyblade series.
Anime Series: A long-running anime produced by OLM, airing globally with different season names (e.g., Burst Evolution, Burst Turbo, Burst Rise, Burst QuadStrike).
Toys: Features complex layered Beyblades with interchangeable parts (Energy Layers, Forge Discs, Performance Tips) across various systems like God Layer, SwitchStrike,
and Dynamite Battle.
Technology: Includes a companion app for digital battling and tracking, enhancing the toy experience.
Seasons & Evolution:
Beyblade Burst (Season 1): Introduces Valt Aoi and his Bey, Valtryek, as they journey to Nationals.
Beyblade Burst Evolution (God): Features the God Layer System and international competitions.
Beyblade Burst Turbo (Chouetsu): Introduces the Cho-Z Layer System and powerful new Beys.
Beyblade Burst Rise (GT): Focuses on the Gatinko Layer System and features new Bladers like Dante Koryu.
Beyblade Burst Surge (Sparking): Introduces the Superking Layer System.
Beyblade Burst QuadDrive/QuadStrike: (Ultimate): The final season, featuring the Dynamite Battle Layer System and new technology.

Beyblade X: The current generation, focusing on speed with metal parts and a "Extreme Line" on the stadium.
Beyblade X is the fourth generation of the popular spinning top toy franchise, featuring fast-paced battles,
metal parts, and a focus on high-speed action in specially designed stadiums with an "Extreme Line"
for unique boosts. The series includes a manga and anime, following protagonists aiming
to become pro bladers, emphasizing strategy and the synergy between powerful "X-Data" Beyblades and
skilled bladers. Key features include customizable three-part Beyblades
(blade, ratchet, bit) and a competitive meta where Attack, Defense, Stamina, and Balance types have strengths and weaknesses.
Key Aspects of Beyblade X
High-Speed Gameplay: The core innovation is the "Extreme Line," a sloped section in the stadium that boosts Beyblades
for intense, fast matches.
Three-Part Customization: Beyblades are built from three components: the metal Blade, the height-determining Ratchet,
and the spinning/burst-resistant Bit, allowing deep customization.
Types: Beyblades fall into Attack, Defense, Stamina, and Balance, with an Attack > Stamina > Defense > Attack cycle
for type advantages.
Anime & Manga: The story follows aspiring bladers, like Ekusu (Ex), training and competing to reach the top, with
characters focusing on strategy and mastering their powerful "X-Data" Beyblades.
Realistic Battles: Compared to some past series, X aims for more grounded battles, focusing on the toy's mechanics
without monster summons, though it still has a competitive toy focus.
What Makes It Different
Focus on Speed: The "X" stadiums and parts are designed for aggressive, high-velocity clashes.
X-Data & "X": The concept of "X-Data" links powerful Beyblades (like Drawn Buster, Wizard Arrow) to elite bladers, driving the narrative.
Competitive Meta: While customizable, the competitive scene revolves around specific parts and combos, with high-tier releases like Shark Scale and Impact Drake being popular.
